# Texas TDLR Security License Requirements: What You Need to Know in 2026

Operating a private security company in Texas without proper TDLR licensing isn't just illegal — it can result in fines up to $10,000 per violation and criminal charges. This guide covers everything you need to stay compliant.

## Who Needs a License?

In Texas, the following require TDLR licensing:

- Security guard companies (Bureau License)
- Individual security officers (Pocket Card)
- Personal protection officers
- Private investigators working in security
- Security consultants
- Alarm system companies

## Company License Types

### Guard Company License (Most Common)

**Requirements:**
- Qualifying manager with 2+ years verifiable security experience
- $10,000 surety bond
- $100,000 liability insurance minimum
- FBI background check (all principals)
- Application fee: $500
- Renewal: Every 2 years

### Investigation Company License

Required if your services include investigations, background checks, or surveillance beyond standard guard duties.

## Individual Guard Licensing Levels

### Level II — Non-Commissioned Security Officer

- Minimum age: 18
- 6 hours of pre-assignment training
- Criminal background check
- Drug screening
- No felony convictions (with exceptions for 20+ year old offenses)

### Level III — Commissioned Security Officer (Armed)

- All Level II requirements
- Additional 40 hours of firearms training
- Firearms proficiency exam
- Psychological evaluation (some employers require)
- Annual firearms requalification

### Level IV — Personal Protection Officer

- All Level III requirements
- Additional specialized training
- Demonstration of personal protection skills
- Advanced firearms certification

## Training Requirements

### Pre-Assignment Training (6 Hours)

All guards must complete before their first shift:
1. Role of private security (1 hour)
2. Legal authority and limitations (1 hour)
3. Emergency procedures (1 hour)
4. Report writing (1 hour)
5. Patrol techniques (1 hour)
6. Professional conduct (1 hour)

### Continuing Education

- Level II: 6 hours annually
- Level III: 6 hours annually + firearms requalification
- Level IV: 6 hours annually + specialized training

## Record Keeping Requirements

TDLR requires companies to maintain:

- Employee training records (keep for 3 years)
- Guard pocket card copies
- Incident reports
- Scheduling records
- Client contracts
- Insurance certificates

## Common TDLR Violations

Avoid these frequent compliance failures:

1. **Deploying guards without pocket cards** — $1,000+ per instance
2. **Expired company insurance** — immediate license suspension
3. **Missing training documentation** — fines per undocumented guard
4. **Unauthorized armed security** — criminal charges possible
5. **Failure to report incidents** — repeated violations escalate penalties

## Application Timeline

| Step | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Gather documents | 1-2 weeks |
| Submit application | Same day |
| Background check processing | 2-4 weeks |
| TDLR review | 2-3 weeks |
| License issued | 1 week |
| **Total** | **6-10 weeks** |

## Keeping Your License Active

- Renew every 2 years (don't let it lapse!)
- Maintain continuous insurance coverage
- Report ownership/management changes within 14 days
- Keep surety bond active
- Complete any required continuing education
