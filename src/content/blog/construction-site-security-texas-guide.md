# Construction Site Security in Texas: Requirements, Costs, and Best Practices

Texas leads the nation in construction spending — over $150 billion annually. With that comes massive theft: the National Equipment Register estimates $1 billion in construction equipment is stolen each year nationally, with Texas as a top state for incidents.

Whether you're a security company looking to win construction contracts or a contractor evaluating security options, this guide covers everything.

## Why Construction Sites Are Targets

### The Perfect Storm

- **Valuable materials** — copper wire, lumber, appliances ($10,000-$500,000 per site)
- **Heavy equipment** — excavators, skid steers, generators ($50,000-$500,000 each)
- **Tools** — power tools disappear constantly ($500-$5,000 per incident)
- **Limited visibility** — sites are often unlit at night
- **Easy access** — perimeter fencing is temporary and easily breached
- **Low prosecution** — stolen materials are hard to identify once removed

### Texas-Specific Factors

- Rapid development creates hundreds of active sites simultaneously
- Urban sprawl means sites are far from police stations (long response times)
- Summer heat means fewer people outside at night to witness theft
- Multi-contractor sites create confusion about who's responsible
- High-value projects in Austin, Houston, Dallas attract organized theft rings

## Types of Construction Site Security

### Security Guards (Most Common)

**Best for**: Active sites with high-value equipment and materials

**Coverage options**:
- Night shift only (6 PM - 6 AM): Most cost-effective
- 24/7 coverage: For high-value or high-risk phases
- Roving patrol: Guard checks multiple sites on a route
- Gate guard: Controls access during work hours

**Typical Texas rates**:
- Unarmed standing guard: $18-25/hour
- Armed guard: $25-35/hour
- Mobile patrol (per check): $15-25/visit
- Supervisor: $25-35/hour

### Mobile Patrol Services

**Best for**: Lower-risk sites, early/late phase construction

**How it works**:
- Guard visits site 2-6 times per night
- Checks perimeter, gates, equipment
- Documents conditions with photos
- Reports concerns immediately

**Cost**: $200-600/night depending on frequency

### Camera Systems

**Best for**: Supplement to guards, not replacement

**Limitations at construction sites**:
- No power in early phases (solar/battery required)
- Constantly changing environment
- Cameras don't prevent theft (only document it)
- Monitoring fees add cost

**Cost**: $500-2,000/month for monitored camera systems

## What Security Companies Should Know

### Winning Construction Contracts

Construction companies and general contractors look for:

1. **Reliability** — guards actually show up, every night
2. **Documentation** — prove the guard was on-site (GPS tracking)
3. **Communication** — immediate alerts for suspicious activity
4. **Flexibility** — scale up/down as project phases change
5. **Professional reporting** — daily site condition reports with photos

### Technology That Wins These Contracts

During your pitch, demonstrate:
- **GPS tracking**: "You'll see exactly when our guard arrives and where they patrol"
- **Checkpoint scanning**: "We place scan points at equipment yards, material storage, and entry gates"
- **Photo documentation**: "Every patrol includes photos of site conditions"
- **Instant alerts**: "You'll get a notification within 5 minutes of any incident"
- **Client portal**: "Log in anytime to see guard activity and reports"

### Pricing Construction Contracts

Construction security pricing considerations:
- **Duration**: Projects last months to years — offer contract discounts
- **Variability**: Early phases need less coverage; demo/final phases need more
- **Access**: Some sites are remote — factor travel time
- **Risk level**: High-value equipment phases warrant premium pricing
- **Multi-site**: Contractors with multiple projects = volume deals

## Guard Post Orders for Construction Sites

### Essential Duties

- Patrol perimeter every 60-90 minutes
- Check all access gates (locked/secured)
- Document equipment inventory (machines present vs. expected)
- Monitor material storage areas
- Challenge unauthorized persons
- Document weather conditions that affect site
- Report safety hazards discovered during patrol
- Log all contractor arrivals/departures (24/7 sites)

### Technology-Enhanced Post Orders

With guard management software:
- Define checkpoint locations at equipment yards, gates, material storage
- Set patrol frequency requirements (e.g., every 90 minutes minimum)
- Require photo documentation at each checkpoint
- Auto-alert if patrol is missed or late
- Client receives automatic daily summary

## OSHA and Legal Considerations

### Site Access Control

OSHA requires controlled access to active construction sites. Security guards can enforce:
- Visitor sign-in procedures
- Safety orientation verification
- PPE compliance at entry
- Unauthorized access prevention
- After-hours access logging

### Documentation for Insurance

Insurance companies may offer reduced premiums for construction sites with:
- Professional security guard service
- Documented nightly patrols (GPS-verified)
- Incident reports maintained for claims
- Access control logs
- Equipment inventory records

## Best Practices

### For Security Companies

1. **Conduct site assessment before bidding** — walk the site, identify vulnerabilities
2. **Propose phased coverage** — match security level to construction phase
3. **Install NFC checkpoints** — prove patrols to the client
4. **Provide daily reports** — photos of site conditions, activity log
5. **Build relationship with site superintendent** — they influence contract renewals
6. **Track equipment inventory** — notice when something's missing before the contractor does

### For Contractors Hiring Security

1. **Require GPS patrol verification** — ensure guards actually patrol
2. **Get client portal access** — see guard activity in real time
3. **Define expectations clearly** — written post orders, patrol frequency, reporting
4. **Review reports weekly** — identify patterns before they become losses
5. **Budget for security** — 0.5-2% of project value for security = $25K-$100K on a $5M project
