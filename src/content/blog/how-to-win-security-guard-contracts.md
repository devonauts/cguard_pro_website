# How to Win Security Guard Contracts: A Sales Guide for Security Companies

Winning contracts is the lifeblood of every security company. Yet most security firms approach sales the same way they did 20 years ago — and wonder why they lose to competitors. Here's how modern security companies win consistently.

## Understanding What Clients Actually Buy

Clients don't buy guard hours. They buy:

- **Peace of mind** — "my property is protected"
- **Risk reduction** — "fewer incidents, lower insurance"
- **Accountability** — "I can see what's happening"
- **Professionalism** — "this makes me look good to my stakeholders"
- **Reliability** — "coverage is always there, without me managing it"

Your proposals and sales conversations should sell these outcomes, not "warm bodies."

## Where to Find Opportunities

### Active Channels

- **RFP databases** — government and large corporate bids
- **Property management companies** — they manage multiple sites
- **Commercial real estate brokers** — know who's leasing and growing
- **General contractors** — construction sites need security
- **Insurance brokers** — they recommend security to reduce premiums

### Passive Channels (Inbound)

- **Google search** — "security guard company [city]" (SEO matters!)
- **Google Business Profile** — optimize for local searches
- **Referrals** — your best source; incentivize them
- **Your website** — professional, with clear CTAs
- **Industry associations** — ASIS, local security associations

### Proactive Outreach

- New construction permits (public record) → offer construction security
- Business park directories → contact property managers directly
- HOA management companies → present at board meetings
- Event venues → seasonal security needs

## Crafting Winning Proposals

### The 3-Part Framework

**1. Show You Understand Their Problem**

Don't start with your company history. Start with:
- "Based on our site assessment, here are the top 3 security risks at your property..."
- "Your current challenges include [specific issues they mentioned]..."
- Reference their industry and specific concerns

**2. Present Your Solution (Not Just Hours)**

Instead of: "We'll provide 2 guards, 24/7, at $22/hour"

Write: "Our security program includes:
- 24/7 dedicated officers at main entrance and loading dock
- Hourly patrol verification with GPS-documented checkpoints
- Real-time incident reporting with photo evidence and instant client notification
- Your dedicated client portal for 24/7 activity visibility
- Monthly security analytics report for your board meetings"

**3. Prove You Can Deliver**

- Case studies from similar properties
- Client testimonials
- Technology demo (client portal screenshot)
- Guard training and certification documentation
- Response time commitments with penalty clauses

### Proposal Must-Haves

- [ ] Executive summary (1 page — decision-makers often only read this)
- [ ] Site-specific security plan (not generic)
- [ ] Technology overview with screenshots
- [ ] Staffing plan with qualifications
- [ ] Training program details
- [ ] Pricing (clear, no hidden fees)
- [ ] Client references
- [ ] Implementation timeline
- [ ] SLA commitments

## Pricing Strategy

### Don't Race to the Bottom

Competing solely on price attracts:
- Price-sensitive clients who will leave for $0.50/hour savings
- Clients who don't value quality (and blame you when things go wrong)
- Margins that don't allow investment in training or technology

### Value-Based Pricing

Justify your rate with tangible differentiators:

| Standard Company | Your Premium Service |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Paper reports, 24-hour delay | Digital reports, instant delivery |
| "Trust us, we're patrolling" | GPS-verified patrol proof |
| Monthly paper summary | 24/7 client portal access |
| Call office during business hours | Real-time mobile communication |
| Basic guard with minimal training | TDLR-certified, regularly trained |

The $2-5/hour premium for these features = $200-500/month per guard = far less than the client portal and technology deliver in value.

### Pricing Structures

- **Hourly rate** (most common): $18-35/hour depending on armed/unarmed, certifications
- **Monthly fixed** (growing trend): Guarantees revenue, clients prefer budget predictability
- **Per-site flat rate**: Best for standard configurations
- **Tiered packages**: Good/Better/Best options (anchor pricing psychology)

## Differentiating Your Company

### The "Same-Same" Problem

To most clients, all security companies look identical:
- Same guard in same uniform
- Same "24/7 professional security" pitch
- Same paper reports
- Same lack of accountability

### How to Stand Out

**1. Technology Demonstration**

In your pitch meeting, open your laptop and show:
- Live guard locations on a map
- Recent patrol completion data
- An incident report with photos
- The client portal they'd get access to

This single demo wins more deals than any other sales tactic.

**2. Site-Specific Security Assessment**

Don't send a generic proposal. Visit the site and deliver:
- Identified vulnerabilities
- Recommended post positions with rationale
- Lighting and camera recommendations
- Patrol route optimization

This shows expertise and investment — most competitors just quote a price.

**3. Transition Plan**

Clients fear switching providers. Remove that fear:
- Day 1-3: Overlap with current provider
- Day 4-7: Supervised solo operation
- Day 8-14: Independent with daily check-ins
- Day 15-30: Standard operations with weekly reviews

**4. Performance Guarantees**

Offer measurable commitments:
- 99%+ shift coverage rate
- Incident reports within 15 minutes
- Monthly analytics report delivery
- 4-hour response to service requests

## Following Up (Most Companies Fail Here)

After submitting a proposal:
- **24 hours**: Email confirming receipt, offer Q&A call
- **3 days**: Follow-up with additional value (relevant case study, article)
- **7 days**: Call to discuss any questions
- **14 days**: Final follow-up with limited-time incentive
- **30 days**: Long-term nurture (monthly newsletter, industry insights)

80% of contracts are won by the company that follows up professionally. Most competitors send a proposal and disappear.
