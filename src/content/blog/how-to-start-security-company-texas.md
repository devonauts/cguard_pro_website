# How to Start a Security Guard Company in Texas: Complete 2026 Guide

Texas is one of the largest markets for private security in the United States. With a growing economy, expanding cities, and increasing demand for professional security services, starting a security guard company in Texas offers tremendous opportunity.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know to launch and grow a successful security company in the Lone Star State.

## Step 1: Understand TDLR Requirements

The Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR) oversees all private security operations in the state. Before you can operate, you'll need:

- **Company License**: Apply for a Guard Company License (Level B) through TDLR
- **Qualifying Manager**: Designate a manager who meets experience and training requirements
- **Background Check**: Complete FBI fingerprint-based background checks
- **Insurance**: Obtain required liability insurance ($100,000 minimum)
- **Bond**: Post a $10,000 surety bond

## Step 2: Choose Your Service Model

### Specialized Services
- Event security

- Loss prevention consulting

## Step 3: Get Licensed and Insured

### TDLR Application Process

1. Complete the Security Bureau Council online application
2. Submit fingerprints for background check ($40 fee)
3. Provide proof of qualifying manager experience
4. Pay application fee ($500 for guard company)
5. Wait for approval (typically 4-6 weeks)

### Insurance Requirements

At minimum, you need:
- General liability: $100,000 per occurrence
- Workers' compensation (required once you hire employees)
- Commercial auto insurance (for patrol vehicles)
- Professional liability (recommended)

## Step 4: Set Up Operations

### Essential Technology Stack

Modern security companies need more than radios and clipboards. Invest in:

- **Guard management software** (scheduling, tracking, reporting)
- **GPS patrol verification** (prove to clients your guards are on-site)
- **Digital incident reporting** (photos, timestamps, automatic notifications)
- **Client portal** (transparency builds trust and wins contracts)

### Hiring Your First Guards

Texas requires all security guards to:
- Be at least 18 years old
- Complete Level II training (non-commissioned) or Level III (commissioned/armed)
- Pass background check and drug screening
- Obtain a TDLR-issued pocket card

## Step 5: Win Your First Contracts

### Target Industries in Texas

Focus your initial sales efforts on:

- **Construction companies** (high demand in growing cities like Austin, Houston, Dallas)
- **HOAs and residential communities** (long-term contracts)
- **Retail centers** (loss prevention needs)

- Unarmed standing guard: $18-28/hour billed rate
- Armed security: $25-45/hour billed rate
- Mobile patrol: $35-65/hour billed rate
- Supervisor: $22-35/hour billed rate
